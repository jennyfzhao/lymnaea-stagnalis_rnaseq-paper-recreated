# RNA-seq Paper Pipeline Comparison

This table includes the paper candidates I investigated enough to evaluate for a reproducible RNA-seq learning workflow. The first three are the downloaded PDFs, now organized into per-paper subfolders under `papers/`.

| Status | Paper | Organism / topic | Public data | Study design | Pipeline / software used in the paper | Good reproduction targets | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Downloaded; best first choice | [Transcriptome analysis provides genome annotation and expression profiles in the central nervous system of *Lymnaea stagnalis* at different ages](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1186/s12864-021-07946-y) | Great pond snail CNS aging / development | [NCBI BioProject PRJNA698985](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/bioproject/?term=PRJNA698985) | 3 age groups: young, adult, old; 4 biological replicates per group; pooled CNS tissue | Illumina NovaSeq 6000 PE150; HISAT2 alignment; StringTie transcript assembly/FPKM/TPM; TransDecoder; BLASTP; HMMER3/hmmscan; Blast2GO; KEGG/GO annotation; featureCounts/Subread; DESeq2; R/ggplot2; Fisher exact test with FDR | Fig. 1 volcano plots, DEG Venn, heatmaps; Fig. 2 GO enrichment; Fig. 3 KEGG summary | Most aligned with your requested “classic” pipeline. Easy to adapt to FastQC/MultiQC, Cutadapt/Trimmomatic, HISAT2/STAR, featureCounts, DESeq2. |
| Downloaded; strong biology choice | [Analysis of sea star larval regeneration reveals conserved processes of whole-body regeneration across the metazoa](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1186/s12915-019-0633-9) | Sea star larval regeneration | [GEO GSE97230](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/geo/query/acc.cgi?acc=GSE97230) | 18 samples total; regenerating anterior fragments, regenerating posterior fragments, and uncut controls; multiple time points; 2 biological replicates | Illumina TruSeq / HiSeq 2500 single-end 50 bp; Trimmomatic; TopHat2 alignment to *Patiria miniata* genome; Cufflinks transcript assembly; MAKER2 gene predictions; HTSeq-count; edgeR quasi-likelihood GLM; R hclust; Blast2GO; reciprocal best BLAST hits; GO enrichment | DEG time-course heatmaps/clusters; GO enrichment panels; regeneration-vs-control DEG lists | More adventurous but interesting. TopHat2 is old, so for learning I would swap in HISAT2 or STAR while keeping the rest conceptually similar. |
| Downloaded; practical applied choice | [Missing Nurse Bees: Early Transcriptomic Switch From Nurse Bee to Forager Induced by Sublethal Imidacloprid](https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/genetics/articles/10.3389/fgene.2021.665927/pdf) | Honey bee pesticide exposure / behavior | [NCBI SRA PRJNA521949](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/sra/PRJNA521949) | Honey bees exposed to sublethal imidacloprid; age/time comparisons | RNA-seq raw reads in SRA; DESeq2 is cited/used for differential expression; paper emphasizes DEG summaries and behavioral interpretation; supplementary tables provide read counts | PCA/cluster-style expression summaries, DEG tables, custom volcano plots, GO-style interpretation | Familiar organism and public reads. Methods are less explicit than the snail/sea-star papers in the article body, so reproduction may need more inference from supplements. |
| Found; not downloaded | [Comparative transcriptomics indicates endogenous differences in detoxification capacity after formic acid treatment between honey bees and varroa mites](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41598-020-79057-9) | Honey bee and *Varroa destructor* mite detoxification | SRA submission reported in paper as `SUB7762334`; exact SRA run lookup needed before use | 54 RNA-seq samples across honey bee / mite treatment groups | FastQC; STAR v2.6.1d; RSEM v1.3.1; kallisto v0.45.0; DESeq2 v1.16.1 in R; PCA; Blast2GO v1.3.3; InterProScan v5.17-56.0; NCBI BLASTP; goseq v1.28.0 | PCA, DEG lists, detoxification-gene plots, GO enrichment | Excellent software match. Less immediately convenient because the article gives an SRA submission ID rather than a clean BioProject accession in the visible methods section. |
| Found; not downloaded | [Comparative Transcriptome Analysis of White and Orange Skin of Clownfish Identifying Differentially Expressed Genes Underlying Pigment Expression](https://www.mdpi.com/2410-3888/11/1/56) | Clown anemonefish white vs orange skin pigmentation | Paper states data available on request; related public clownfish RNA-seq exists, but not clearly this dataset | 3 male *A. ocellaris* individuals; white and orange skin regions | FastQC v0.11.8; STAR v2.7.0f to Ensembl release 104 genome; GenomicAlignments v1.30.0 in R for counts; PLINK PCA; DESeq2 with individual effects; BLAST v2.12.0; Ensembl BioMart; STRING v11; DAVID v6.8 GO enrichment | PCA, volcano plot, DEG table, PPI/GO figures | Very interesting and familiar organism, but not a good primary choice unless the authors provide the raw reads. |
| Found; not downloaded | [Comparative RNA-seq Analysis in the Unsequenced Axolotl: The Oncogene Burst Highlights Early Gene Expression in the Blastema](https://journals.plos.org/ploscompbiol/article?id=10.1371/journal.pcbi.1002936) | Axolotl limb regeneration | Paper points to an axolotl-omics website; accession lookup needed before use | Limb blastema time course from 0 h to 28 days after amputation; pooled animals per time point | De novo axolotl transcript/contig approach; transcript quantification without a reference genome; mapping contigs to human genes; edgeR for time-course DE; qPCR validation; functional analysis using human gene-set mapping | Time-course expression plots, DE gene sets, pathway/category enrichment | Fascinating biology, but older and less ideal for a standard modern FastQC -> align/pseudoalign -> count -> DESeq2 teaching pipeline. |
